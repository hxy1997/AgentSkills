# Business Potential Framework

Use this file when judging the business, org, and role-level upside.

## Signals of internal importance

Positive:

- business is mentioned in earnings calls, annual letters, major launches, or founder interviews
- hiring remains active across adjacent roles
- role touches core company metrics, flagship products, or strategic AI initiatives
- team collaborates with revenue, platform, or high-visibility product groups
- senior leader sponsorship is visible

Negative:

- business appears only in isolated recruiting language
- repeated pivots without clear product traction
- role is framed as support with inflated title
- team seems isolated from company priorities
- public messaging and hiring scope do not match

## Signals of product competitiveness

Check:

- user need clarity
- product quality or distribution advantage
- ecosystem lock-in
- speed of iteration
- pricing power
- defensibility versus direct competitors

Questions:

- Is the company the category leader, a credible challenger, or late follower?
- Is the product winning because of product strength, channel power, or low price?
- Are rivals better funded or shipping faster?

## Signals of org stability

Positive:

- clear reporting line
- stable leadership tenure
- measurable team charter
- coherent hiring story

Negative:

- frequent leadership turnover
- role scope depends on unresolved reorg
- JD mixes strategy, delivery, operations, and firefighting with no prioritization
- unclear success metrics

## Signals of role realism

High realism:

- responsibilities are concrete
- success metrics are visible
- stakeholders are specific
- role scope matches title and level

Low realism:

- broad ownership with no authority
- title implies strategy but JD is mostly execution
- role spans too many unrelated functions
- outcome metrics are absent

## Signals of growth and transferability

Prefer roles that offer:

- ownership over important business levers
- exposure to scarce workflows or hard-to-copy experience
- collaboration with strong operators or technical leaders
- artifacts that can become future resume proof points

Avoid roles that likely produce:

- repetitive coordination without strategic visibility
- highly company-specific work with weak external value
- support burden that crowds out learning
