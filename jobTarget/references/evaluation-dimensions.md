# Evaluation Dimensions

Use this file as the canonical checklist when building the scorecard.

## 1. Input completeness

Record:

- JD provided or missing
- Resume provided or missing
- Company or team context provided or missing
- Candidate priorities provided or missing
- Blocking gaps
- Minimal follow-up questions

Do not issue a strong recommendation if two or more core inputs are missing.

## 2. Business potential

Judge whether the underlying business is worth joining.

Rate:

- `high`: core or rising business with visible investment and strategic relevance
- `medium`: stable but not obviously strategic, or growth exists with mixed signals
- `low`: edge business, weak strategic importance, or fading momentum

Look for:

- resource concentration
- executive attention
- product launch cadence
- hiring continuity
- monetization clarity
- market timing

## 3. Company and product competitiveness

Judge whether the company's offering can compete in its segment.

Check:

- product positioning
- distribution strength
- monetization
- retention or ecosystem advantages
- direct competitor posture
- whether the company is leader, challenger, follower, or laggard

## 4. Org and team signals

Judge whether the org can support healthy execution.

Check:

- team position in the org chart
- leader authority
- real headcount need versus backfill ambiguity
- reorg history
- cross-functional leverage
- likely decision speed

## 5. Community sentiment

Treat this as supporting evidence, not decisive proof.

Summarize:

- source type
- date
- recurring themes
- positive signals
- negative signals
- confidence level
- bias warnings

## 6. Decision-changing dimensions

These dimensions should usually be checked explicitly because they often change the recommendation more than generic company prestige does.

Assess:

- actual work intensity
- HC type and org stability
- work-content mix
- AI or Agent relevance
- performance, compensation, and commute

### 6.1 Actual work intensity

Check:

- average end time
- late-night meeting frequency
- oncall or incident duty
- weekend support
- peak-season or delivery-cycle pressure

### 6.2 HC and org stability

Check:

- new role versus backfill
- predecessor exit reason
- reporting line clarity
- reorg frequency
- leadership stability

### 6.3 Work-content mix

Check:

- product or platform building share
- business request share
- firefighting or operational support share
- customer delivery or customization share

### 6.4 AI or Agent relevance

Check:

- whether AI or Agent work is core roadmap or optional exploration
- dedicated ownership or only side-project status
- expected share of weekly work
- whether success will create durable AI career capital

### 6.5 Performance, compensation, and commute

Check:

- KPI logic and ranking pressure
- compensation structure and upside
- trial-period expectations
- office location and commute burden

## 7. Role realism

Judge whether the JD describes a real, valuable, and internally important role.

Look for:

- concrete scope
- ownership clarity
- impact language versus operational chores
- mismatch between title and described work
- suspicious breadth without authority

## 8. Candidate fit

Rate:

- `high`: most hard requirements and context match
- `medium`: some direct matches plus transferable strengths
- `low`: large requirement gap or missing domain credibility

Assess:

- direct experience match
- transferable accomplishments
- level mismatch
- business context match
- narrative strength for interviews

## 9. Growth and transferability

Judge whether the role builds durable career capital.

Check:

- skill scarcity
- exposure to core metrics
- decision ownership
- visibility to senior stakeholders
- future portability across companies

## 10. Reward-to-risk ratio

Consider both upside and cost.

Check:

- compensation upside
- title or scope improvement
- learning curve
- intensity and burnout risk
- performance pressure
- trial-period risk

## 11. Opportunity cost

Compare against realistic alternatives rather than evaluating in isolation.

Check:

- current role baseline
- competing interview pipelines or offers
- marketability gained or lost
- switching cost

## 12. Interview prep priorities

Split the prep into:

- hard gaps that need evidence or study
- likely deep-dive questions
- stories that should be strengthened
- reverse questions to validate unknowns

## 13. Risk list

For each risk, record:

- label
- severity: `high`, `medium`, or `low`
- evidence
- confidence
- validation method

## 14. Additional information to collect

For each high-uncertainty role, record:

- which missing information matters most
- how it would change the recommendation if positive
- how it would change the recommendation if negative

Do not treat all missing data equally. Prioritize the unknowns that are closest to the candidate's stated priorities.

## 15. Final recommendation

Use one label only:

- `建议冲`
- `可继续推进但需重点验证`
- `谨慎推进`
- `不建议`

The recommendation must summarize:

- why this role may be worth pursuing
- what could go wrong
- what the candidate should verify next
