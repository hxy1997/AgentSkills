# Community Source Reliability

Use this file when interpreting content from Maimai, Nowcoder, Kanzhun, Boss, Zhihu, forums, blogs, or anonymous review sites.

## Default posture

- Treat community content as weak-to-medium evidence.
- Use it to detect patterns, not to prove a claim.
- Prefer repeated themes across multiple dates and authors.

## Confidence ladder

### High confidence

- Official page, filing, or interview confirms the same point.
- Multiple independent public sources align on the same event.

### Medium confidence

- Several community posts describe the same issue with consistent details.
- Timing aligns with public company events such as reorgs or layoffs.

### Low confidence

- Single anonymous post
- Emotional rant without specifics
- Screenshots or hearsay without source trail

## Common biases

- unhappy employees speak louder than average employees
- candidates rejected by the team may over-index on negative conclusions
- old posts may describe a different leader or org
- high-intensity teams often attract extreme sentiment in both directions

## Handling rules

- Quote themes, not rumor details.
- Record the date and platform for each notable signal.
- Mark anonymous claims as `community signal`.
- If evidence conflicts, present the contradiction instead of averaging it away.

## Site-specific hints

### Maimai

- Useful for org rumors, manager style, and recent mobility.
- High rumor density; verify before treating as fact.

### Nowcoder

- Useful for interview process, candidate experience, and common assessment focus.
- Often candidate-centric and may underrepresent on-the-job reality.

### Kanzhun and Boss

- Useful for repeated complaints about overtime, management, and pay fairness.
- Review freshness matters a lot.

### Zhihu and blogs

- Useful for thematic writeups and context, but quality varies sharply by author.

## When to stop searching

Stop broad community search when:

- you already have 3 or more recurring themes
- new results are repetitive
- evidence quality is falling

At that point, convert remaining uncertainty into reverse questions from [interview-question-patterns.md](./interview-question-patterns.md).
